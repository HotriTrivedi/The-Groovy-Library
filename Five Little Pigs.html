Five Little Pigs

About the Book

"Five Little Pigs" is a classic detective novel written by Agatha Christie, first published in 1942. The story revolves around the murder of a wealthy painter, Amyas Crale, who was poisoned with coniine. His wife, Caroline Crale, was convicted of the murder and died in prison. Sixteen years later, their daughter, Carla Lemarchant, seeks the help of the famous detective Hercule Poirot to clear her mother's name. Poirot meticulously re-examines the case, interviewing the five people present at the time of the murder, each of whom has their own version of events. Through his ingenious deduction, Poirot uncovers hidden motives and secrets, ultimately revealing the surprising truth behind the murder.
