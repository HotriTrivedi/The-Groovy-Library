The Shining

About the Book

"The Shining" is a chilling and iconic novel written by master of horror Stephen King. Set in the eerie and isolated Overlook Hotel, nestled in the Colorado Rockies, the story follows Jack Torrance, a struggling writer and recovering alcoholic, who accepts a job as the winter caretaker of the hotel. Jack moves in with his wife, Wendy, and young son, Danny, hoping to find peace and solitude to work on his writing. However, as the winter snows trap them in the hotel, disturbing supernatural forces begin to manifest, awakening long-buried secrets and driving Jack to the brink of madness. With its spine-tingling suspense, psychological depth, and unforgettable characters, "The Shining" is a masterpiece of horror literature that continues to terrify readers and captivate audiences worldwide.

About the Author

Stephen King is a prolific and bestselling author known for his unparalleled contributions to the horror genre. Born in Portland, Maine, King began writing at a young age and gained widespread acclaim with his first novel, "Carrie," published in 1974. Since then, he has written numerous bestsellers, including "The Shining," "It," and "Misery," among others. With his vivid imagination, keen understanding of human nature, and mastery of suspense, King has earned a reputation as one of the most influential and enduring storytellers of our time. His works have been adapted into countless films, television series, and stage productions, solidifying his status as a cultural icon and a towering figure in the world of literature.
